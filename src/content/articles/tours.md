<div style="font-size: 20px; font-weight: bold; color: #0148a0; border-bottom: 2px solid #eaeaea; padding-bottom: 8px; margin-bottom: 16px;">1. Badaling Great Wall</div>
<p style="margin: 0 0 16px 0;">The Badaling Great Wall, located in Beijing&rsquo;s Yanqing District about 70 kilometers northwest of the city center, is the most famous and best-preserved section of China&rsquo;s iconic Great Wall. Constructed primarily during the Ming Dynasty (1368&ndash;1644), Badaling was strategically rebuilt in 1505 and served as a crucial military defense point guarding the Juyong Pass, the northern gateway to Beijing. Its name &ldquo;Badaling&rdquo; literally means &ldquo;giving access to every direction,&rdquo; reflecting the radiating mountain ridges that made it an ideal defensive position.</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 16px 0;">Stretching dramatically across steep mountain ridges, Badaling offers visitors breathtaking panoramic views of the wall snaking endlessly into the distance, especially spectacular in autumn when the surrounding hills are covered in red and golden foliage. The section open to tourists features wide, well-restored stone walkways, towering watchtowers, and beacon towers every few hundred meters. The highest point, the North Eighth Watchtower, stands at 888 meters above sea level and provides an unforgettable vantage point.</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 24px 0;">Badaling is fully equipped for international visitors: cable car access is available for those who prefer not to climb the steep sections, and the site includes museums, a circular-screen theater showing the history of the Great Wall, and the Great Wall Museum nearby. It is easily reachable by direct train (S2 line from Beijing North Railway Station) or comfortable tourist buses. As the first section of the Great Wall opened to the public in 1957 and visited by hundreds of world leaders and dignitaries, Badaling remains the quintessential Great Wall experience&mdash;majestic, historically significant, and visually stunning&mdash;making it a perfect highlight for any international conference delegation visiting Beijing.</p>

<div style="font-size: 20px; font-weight: bold; color: #0148a0; border-bottom: 2px solid #eaeaea; padding-bottom: 8px; margin-bottom: 16px;">2. Summer Palace (Yiheyuan)</div>
<p style="margin: 0 0 16px 0;">The Summer Palace, known in Chinese as Yiheyuan, is universally regarded as the finest and most perfectly preserved imperial garden in China. Located 15 kilometers northwest of central Beijing in Haidian District, this UNESCO World Heritage Site covers 3.009 square kilometers, three-quarters of which is water, dominated by the serene Kunming Lake and the gently rising Longevity Hill (Wanshou Shan).</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 16px 0;">Originally built in 1750 by Emperor Qianlong as a luxurious retreat for the imperial family to escape Beijing&rsquo;s summer heat, the palace was largely destroyed during the Second Opium War in 1860 and magnificently rebuilt in 1888 under the direction of Empress Dowager Cixi, who famously diverted navy funds for its restoration. The garden masterfully combines natural landscape with classical Chinese architecture, embodying the philosophical principle of harmony between man and nature.</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 16px 0;">Highlights include the 728-meter-long Covered Corridor, decorated with over 14,000 exquisite Suzhou-style paintings; the magnificent Seventeen-Arch Bridge leading to South Lake Island; the elegant Marble Boat symbolizing stability; and the grand Hall of Benevolence and Longevity where emperors once handled state affairs. At the top of Longevity Hill stands the towering Buddhist Incense Pavilion and the Sea of Wisdom Temple adorned with colorful glazed tiles.</p>
<p style="margin: 0 0 24px 0;">In spring and summer, the vast lotus blooms on Kunming Lake create a poetic atmosphere, while winter brings ice-skating and stunning snow-covered traditional roofs. Visitors can take a dragon-shaped boat across the lake or simply stroll along the shaded pathways. With multilingual audio guides, English signage, and easy access by subway Line 4 (Beigongmen or Xiyuan stations), the Summer Palace offers international guests a peaceful yet majestic glimpse into imperial Chinese aesthetics and garden design&mdash;an ideal relaxing and culturally enriching half-day visit after conference sessions.</p>
